Protected Swings
Manipulation
What is a manipulation protected swing?
Manipulation is the core concept for applying protected swings to the charts.
Highs and Lows
Manipulation occurs upon the false run through a high or low which opposes the directional
draw. Trading below a relevant low then quickly returning back above is a bullish
manipulation. Trading above a relevant high then quickly returning back below is a bearish
manipulation.
How do you know which high or low is relevant? This is what you will learn throughout the
lessons within this course. However, understanding the basic concept of manipulation is a
requirement before moving on to learning how to establish narrative.

=== PAGE 6 ===
Manipulation
Every expansion begins with a manipulation. It is a required component to protected swings
as all other parts of the process are rendered ineffective without it occurring first. If there is
no manipulation, then there is no participation.
Manipulation which holds in the market will occur at both relevant prices and relevant
times. Each element is equally important when it comes to understanding true manipulation.

=== PAGE 7 ===
By the end of this course, you will understand exactly where to look, when to look for it,
and what timeframe to look on.
Confirmation
Change in state of delivery is used as the confirmation for a manipulation. To confirm a
manipulation of a relevant low, the confirmation is the close above the opening price of the
highest downclose candle which ran into the low. To confirm a manipulation of a relevant

high, the confirmation is the close below the opening price of the lowest upclose candle
which ran into the high.
The manipulation is only an anticipation prior to the change in state of delivery. Upon the
close for confirmation, the manipulation changes from an anticipation to an actionable
signature which can be applied to all respective timeframes.
Invalidation

The point of invalidation for a manipulation varies depending on the timeframe being
applied.
For high and intermediate timeframes, the invalidation is a close through the fifty percent
level from the highest candle body to the lowest candle body. These timeframes are used to
frame narrative and confirmation which is favored to see respect of the upper fifty percent
when bullish and the lower fifty percent when bearish.
For low timeframes, the invalidation is trading through the swing point that was considered
protected which is the manipulation swing low when bullish and the manipulation swing
high when bearish. This timeframe is used for entries and the protected swing point is where
stop losses are placed.

Characteristics
When considering the characteristics of a true manipulation, price should be moving with a
certain level of urgency upon trading through the high or low. This can appear differently
depending on the timeframe.
On the higher timeframes, closing above a high is considered a negative condition for a
bearish manipulation and closing below a low is considered a negative condition for a

protocols for each timeframe.

Divergence
What is a divergence protected swing?
Divergence is a confluence tool for a manipulation.
Confluence
The only scenario that a manipulation is not required on the pair being traded is if there is a
divergence present on a correlated pair. If price falls short of a high or low which was
expected to be manipulated, then observe a correlated pair for a potential divergence. If an
expected high or low is manipulated, then observe a correlated for a potential divergence to
add confluence.
Both scenarios result in the opportunity to study a correlated pair. There will either be
confirmation that the manipulation has failed or a confluence will be provided for a valid
manipulation. Intermarket analysis can be simple, yet effective when applied properly.

=== PAGE 13 ===
Confirmation
When trading the pair with the failure swing, the change in state of delivery is defined
differently. To confirm a divergence on a failure swing low, the confirmation is the close
above the opening price of the highest downclose candle which formed the higher low. To
confirm a divergence on a failure swing high, the confirmation is the close below the
opening price of the lowest upclose candle which formed the lower high.

=== PAGE 14 ===
To add an optional, but extra layer of assurance, waiting for a change in state of delivery on
both correlated pairs is the highest quality of confirmation. This ensures that there is
agreement between each correlated market that the protected swing has formed.
Protection
In a similar manner to the change in state of delivery, the protected swing is defined
differently on the pair with the failure swing. For a divergence with a failure swing low, the

=== PAGE 15 ===
protected swing is the lower low which was formed previously. For a divergence with a
failure swing high, the protected swing is the higher high which was formed previously.
The reason for this is because the divergence between the correlated pairs remains intact
until price trades through what is defined as the protected swing. Divergence is not the only
indicator for relative strength or weakness which means that this logic can prevent
unnecessary and premature removals from the market.

Continuation
What is a continuation protected swing?
Opposing close candles are used for protected swings during a phase of expansion.
Opposing Close Candles
Following the initial manipulation which begins the expansion, opposing close candles are
expected hold price throughout the continuation to the objective. Downclose candles will
support price when bullish and upclose candles will resist price when bearish.
This is a signature that is exclusive to expansion markets. Without a narrative for expansion,
there is no consistency to this expectation.

=== PAGE 17 ===
Confirmation
The same logic of the change in state of delivery can be applied when using confirmations
for opposing close candles within a continuation.
To confirm a downclose candle protected low within a continuation, the confirmation is the
close above the opening price of the highest downclose candle which started the opposing
run lower. To confirm an upclose candle protected swing within a continuation, the

=== PAGE 18 ===
confirmation is the close below the opening price of the lowest upclose candle which started
the opposing run higher.
Selection
How do you know which opposing close candles should hold price? There are three
scenarios to look for during a continuation.

=== PAGE 19 ===
The first is a wide series of opposing close candles with bulky candle bodies. Upon the
close through for confirmation, this is a scenario that a protected swing is formed at the
other side of the series of opposing close candles. Since price does not make deep
retracements during expansions, it is the expectation that the wide series of opposing close
candles will hold price.
The second is a single or series of opposing close candles which either ran out a short term
swing or was in respect to previous opposing close candles. Upon the close through for
confirmation, this is another scenario that a protected swing is formed at the other side of
the of opposing close candles. If price is already delivering off a relevant point within the
continuation, then it becomes unlikely that the market will need to return back to that level
before expanding again.
The third is a single or series of opposing close candles with a small ranged candle body and
wick. Upon the close through, this does not serve as a confirmation for a protected swing. It
is expected that price will run out a short term swing before continuing the expansion. The
run on the short term swing creates the valid protected swing within this scenario of a
continuation.

being applied.
For high and intermediate timeframes, the invalidation is a close through the fifty percent
level from the highest candle body to the lowest candle body. These timeframes are used to

considered protected. This timeframe is used for entries and the protected swing point is
where stop losses are placed. Never place a stop loss internally within the opposing close
candles on the entry timeframe. Always at a swing high or low.

Daily Chart
Trade Away
What is the first step of the process on the daily chart?
Study the daily chart to determine if there is a point to trade away from.
Protected Swing
When considering protected swings to trade away from on the daily chart, there are only
two conditions to concern yourself with.
The first is a manipulation at a daily high or low. It is important to determine where and
when the most recent manipulation occurred in order to trade away from it. Always avoid
trading back into the most recent point of manipulation as the expansion is most likely to
occur in the other direction.
The second is a daily opposing close candle. If the market is delivering from a previous
manipulation and has a close through an opposing close candle within the continuation, then
that forms a protected swing which should be traded away from. The expectation is that it
will hold price as the market continues to trade towards the objective.

Trade Towards
What is the second step of the process on the daily chart?
Study the daily chart to determine if there is a point to trade towards.
Failure Swings
The draw to identify on the daily chart are failure swings which are opposing the previous
protected swing. What is a failure swing? Failure swings are considered unprotected as they
are highs and lows which failed to be manipulated. This creates areas on the chart that the
market will want to trade towards and through without any amount of resistance.
Failure swing highs form an initial high with consecutive lower highs which follow it.
Failure swing lows form an initial low with consecutive higher lows which follow it. The
initial swing point is the high or low which should have been manipulated prior to trading
away from it.

=== PAGE 25 ===
Potential Reversal
The opposing draw should both act as a draw on price and an area which a future reversal
could be framed from. When a reversal can be framed at the point being used as the
opposing draw, this ensures that you are targeting the final point where the continuation
should end. Seek confirmations and trade away from the most recent manipulation into the
opposing draw until shown otherwise.

Price Delivery
This section will refine the understanding of daily price delivery cycles.
Expansion
The daily chart is always in one continuous cycle which revolves around expansion.
Price moves from an expansion, to a new phase of price delivery, then into another
expansion to repeat the cycle. This can be used to tell you both when to stop
trading continuation days and also when to reestablish a previously formed bias.
How do we know when a phase of expansion will end? There are two ways.
Three consecutive daily expansions in the same direction.
Two consecutive daily expansions into a significant objective.
Following both maximum expansion signatures on the daily chart, avoid trading
continuations in the previous direction and anticipate a new phase of price delivery
to take place. While the initial expectation is always to look back into the range,
protected swings can be used to determine how the next phase should trade.

=== PAGE 28 ===
Process
Before getting into using protected swings to determine how the next phase should
trade, it is important to understand how to simplify this process for yourself. Should
you attempt to trade the day following the end of a daily expansion phase? There
are certain levels which would determine this choice.

=== PAGE 29 ===
The first is the general understanding that it will always be easiest to allow the day
following the end of a daily expansion phase to trade without your participation.
How the following daily candle prints on the chart will refine down the possible
scenarios that can be framed.
If it forms or confirms a reversal with a protected swing, then it is a continuation
back into the range.
If it remains internal without a protected swing, then focus on the external swing
points for manipulation.
If it expands in either direction without a protected swing, then more waiting is
required.
The second part is the understanding that the day following is best to be traded
when it is in alignment with a weekly range logic. While it still requires confirmation
between one of the three frameworks, this added confluence allows for a more
likely trade on the following day back into the range. Outside of this signature, it is
often best to let the following day trade without you in order to be used as a filter
for the next phase of price delivery.
Consolidation
When is the new phase of price delivery a consolidation? Following a daily
expansion, a protected swing fails to form on either side of the market while a
range is established.
As there is no protected swing on either side of the market within the internal
range, annotate the range high and low. The market must form a protected swing
on one side of the range before participating again and the extremes of the range is
where they will form. Does price move within the internal range? Yes, some will
attempt to trade it. Is it offering a defined trading edge in that condition? No, so we
will avoid it.
While the consolidation cannot be predicted in advance, the development and
process of applying protected swings keeps you out of the market until it can be
identified.

=== PAGE 30 ===
Consolidations are a neutral state of the market. This means that the protocol does
not change depending on which side of the external range is engaged first. There
are two ways to frame the end of the consolidation into a new daily phase of
expansion.
1. Manipulation of the external range
2. Divergence with a correlated pair which manipulated the external range

=== PAGE 31 ===
Both scenarios involve a manipulation of one side of the range to then expand into
the opposing side of the range. For determining whether to trade the manipulation
day or to wait for the continuation, use the lesson on large versus small wicks from
the Foundation Model course.
Following the manipulation of one side of the consolidation range, the opposing
side of the range becomes the initial draw. However, determining whether it is the
final objective for the expansion or if price should continue expanding through that

=== PAGE 32 ===
level requires added context as the market is no longer in a neutral condition. Look
for what you should Trade Away from and where you should Trade Towards.
Reversal
When is the new phase of price delivery a reversal? Following a maximum
expansion, a protected swing is formed with an opposing draw.
Since the expectation at the end of a daily expansion to the upside is that the
market will not continue higher from that point, the formation of a protected high
becomes a potential selling opportunity back into the range. Since the expectation
at the end of a daily expansion to the downside is that the market will not continue
lower from that point, the formation of a protected low becomes a potential buying
opportunity back into the range.
This can occur in one of three ways; one for each type of protected swing.
1. If the final expansion day creates a large wick with an hourly reversal, then seek
trades off the internal opposing close candles back into the range
2. If the final expansion day does not reverse to form a protected swing, then wait
for that daily swing point to be manipulated for framework to trade back into the
range.
3. If the final expansion day does not reverse to form a protected swing, then a
divergence at that daily swing point is a confluence for manipulation on a
correlated pair to trade back into the range.

Retracement
When is the new phase of price delivery a retracement? Following a maximum
expansion, price moves back into the range with a protected swing where the
previous expansion delivered from.
While there will be instances where trades are taken with the intention of a reversal,
but develop into a retracement, there is a specific framework to seek. The ideal
scenario to frame a daily retracement following a previous phase of expansion is for
the opposing move back into the range to be delivering away from an unprotected
swing and into a protected swing.

Intraweek Development
How is the development of the weekly range used in relation to the
daily chart?
Intraweek development pairs alongside the understanding of daily price delivery.
Weekly Opening Price
When it comes time to observe the weekly range at the new week open, the first step is to
draw out the weekly opening price; this is 18:00 on Sunday for Eastern Standard Time
(EST).
If a protected high forms above the weekly opening price, the weekly range is framed to be
bearish until an opposing objective below the weekly opening price is reached or the
narrative is invalidated. If a protected low is formed below the weekly opening price, the
weekly range is framed to be bullish until an opposing objective above the weekly opening
price is reached or the narrative is invalidated.
Everything in the weekly range is occurring in relation to this time and price level.

With a bullish expectation going into the new week open, either an initial run below the

Expansion Week
What is the formation of an expansion weekly range?
This is a refined weekly range logic of a classic expansion weekly profile template.
Formation
Bullish expansion week:
Following the week open, a protected low is formed on either Monday or Tuesday below
the weekly opening price. This protected low should form after a relatively shallow run
below the weekly opening price as the large wick rule applies the same to weekly candles as
it does to daily candles when considering expansion profiles. Once the protected low is
confirmed, Tuesday through Thursday can be expansion days to the upside towards the
opposing draw. Thursday and Friday can offer selling opportunities back into the weekly
range once the opposing draw is reached. While a Thursday counter-trend is dependent on if
the weekly objective is reached or not, Friday should never be traded as a continuation to
the upside when there is an expansion off of a Monday or Tuesday low of week.
Bearish expansion week:
Following the week open, a protected high is formed on either Monday or Tuesday above
the weekly opening price. This protected high should form after a relatively shallow run
above the weekly opening price as the large wick rule applies the same to weekly candles as
it does to daily candles when considering expansion profiles. Once the protected high is
confirmed, Tuesday through Thursday can be expansion days to the downside towards the
opposing draw. Thursday and Friday can offer buying opportunities back into the weekly
range once the opposing draw is reached. While a Thursday counter-trend is dependent on if
the weekly objective is reached or not, Friday should never be traded as a continuation to
the downside when there is an expansion off of a Monday or Tuesday high of week.

Reversal Week
What is the formation of a reversal weekly range?
This is a refined weekly range logic of a midweek reversal weekly profile template.
Formation
Bullish reversal week:
Following the week open, a protected low is formed on Wednesday below the weekly
opening price. This protected low can form either shallow or deep below the weekly
opening price as the large wick rule does not apply to reversal profiles. Once the protected
low is confirmed, Wednesday through Friday can be expansion days to the upside towards
the opposing draw. While the opposing draw can be reached on either Thursday or Friday,
there is never a selling opportunity back into the range after expanding from the Wednesday
low as the expectation is for Friday to close near the high of the week.
Bearish reversal week:
Following the week open, a protected high is formed on Wednesday above the weekly
opening price. This protected high can form either shallow or deep above the weekly
opening price as the large wick rule does not apply to reversal profiles. Once the protected
high is confirmed, Wednesday through Friday can be expansion days to the downside
towards the opposing draw. While the opposing draw can be reached on either Thursday or
Friday, there is never a buying opportunity back into the range after expanding from the
Wednesday high as the expectation is for Friday to close near the low of the week.

Range Week
What is the formation of a range week?
This is a refined weekly range logic of a consolidation reversal weekly profile template.
Formation
Bullish range week:
Following the week open, Monday through Wednesday lack the formation of a protected
swing on either side of the market. There are three ways the bullish range week can form in
relation to the external weekly range low which must first be manipulated to offer a trade
opportunity. First, Thursday reverses from the external weekly range low and Friday is a
continuation to the upside to an opposing draw. Second, Thursday reverses from the
external weekly range low and achieves the opposing draw which sets the framework for a
selling opportunity back into the range on Friday. Third, Thursday also lacks the formation
of a protected swing and Friday reverses from the external weekly range low.
Bearish range week:
Following the week open, Monday through Wednesday lack the formation of a protected
swing on either side of the market. There are three ways the bearish range week can form in
relation to the external weekly range high which must first be manipulated to offer a trade
opportunity. First, Thursday reverses from the external weekly range high and Friday is a
continuation to the downside to an opposing draw. Second, Thursday reverses from the
external weekly range high and achieves the opposing draw which sets the framework for a
buying opportunity back into the range on Friday. Third, Thursday also lacks the formation
of a protected swing and Friday reverses from the external weekly range high.

Daily Candle
How should an expansive daily candle form?
This understanding is the foundation to confirming and executing an anticipated
trade idea.
Expansion Daily Candle
While there are three ways to frame a weekly candle, an expansion is always the
intention for a daily candle. This is the only condition that is of interest to trade
within as it provides a repeatable intraday framework, brings respect to opposing
close candles, and allows for larger profit opportunity. Understanding the
characteristics of an expansion daily candle and how it is expected to form is the
starting point to determining if and how to be positioning trades on these days.
Bullish expansion daily candle:
Following the daily candle open, there is a shallow run below the daily opening
price. A small lower wick on an expected upclose expansion day is a consistent rule
across all markets. Any move far below the opening price prior to reversing is a sign
the expansion day to the upside is invalidated. After an intraday reversal is formed
shallow below the daily opening price, the expansion can begin to the upside. It is
expected that the daily candle will close near the high of the day. This is why it is
unfavorable to attempt counter-trending with sells within a bullish daily candle
expansion.
1. Initial shallow run below the daily opening price
2. Expansion to the upside from the point of reversal
3. Daily closure near the high of the day

Intraday Reversal
What is the framework for an intraday reversal?
The intraday reversal is the protected swing of the daily range.
Reversal Day
Since the framework for the intraday reversal on reversal days is based on the daily chart
and weekly range, it will not be covered here as it was in previous sections. An intraday
reversal for a reversal day is the same as the intraweek reversal.
While the lesson on framing intraday reversals for reversal days is not covered in this
section, the lesson on timeframe selection for confirming intraday reversals does apply for
reversal days.
Continuation Day
Framework for continuation days should be determined on the hourly chart. After price
delivers from a daily protected swing in alignment with the weekly range, the only
signatures to focus on for the continuation days are hourly opposing close candles and
hourly swing points.
Since the established higher timeframe narrative is expected to bring price in the intended
direction, the continuation days simply need to give confirmation in alignment with it.
Read below to learn about framing the intraday reversal for continuation days:
Scenario One
If the anticipated continuation day opens following the confirmation of an opposing close
candle, then the follow intraday reversal should form in respect to it since it is acting as a
protected swing.
For a bullish continuation day in this scenario, annotate the opening price of the highest
downclose candle. For a bearish continuation day in this scenario, annotate the opening

Entry Application
Change in State of Delivery
How is a change in state of delivery applied for entries?
This is an entry which occurs at the anticipated high or low of day.
Application
Applying a change in state of delivery introduces a different perspective on how
entries are understood. Instead of viewing the lower timeframes in entry models, it
is to be viewed as a way to position yourself with a previously determined bias
without being removed from the market as it plays out. All the work is done within
the previous steps of the process. When the narrative is properly aligned, the
change in state of delivery will work as intended as a byproduct.
For a bullish change in state of delivery, the close above the opening price of the
highest downclose candle at the point of intraday reversal confirms a protected low.
For a bearish change in state of delivery, the close below the opening price of the
lowest upclose candle at the point of intraday reversal confirms a protected high.
Since the close creates the protected swing, an entry can be taken on the open of
the following candle.
Once positioned, the expectation is that the stop loss is placed at the protected
swing and the higher timeframe narrative will bring the trade to the objectives
without removing you from the market. The only purpose is to be positioned with
proper risk to reward which means a retest of the opposing close candles is not a
required component to the entry.

Opposing Close Candles
How are opposing close candles applied for entries?
Opposing close candles are used during the period of continuation.
Logic
Why do opposing close candles work? The market does not see lower timeframe signatures.
Opposing close candles being respected on the timeframe of entry is only a byproduct of the
higher timeframe market condition. An expansion market is the key.
When price is expanding, opposing close candles become a simple and effective signature
which is respected with high consistency. Focus solely on downclose candles when in a
bullish continuation and upclose candles when in a bearish continuation. The intraday
continuation is the body of the daily candle and the body of the daily candle is the
expansion.

Application
Following the confirmation of the intraday reversal, the market enters an intraday
continuation into the anticipated direction. Opposing close candles are applied the same in
regards to entry logic as previously discussed for the change in state of delivery. However,
opposing close candles in a continuation also offer the understanding for pyramid entries
and trailing a stop loss on an open position.

The 15-minute chart is the timeframe which provides the most consistent application.
Within an expansion day, closes through 15-minute opposing close candles during the
continuation is a significant signature. This can offer a continuation entry, a pyramid entry
from a previous position, or a quality level to trail a stop loss.
The 5-minute chart is the timeframe to be used for the same purposes, but in a market which
is developing at a faster pace. While it is best to always start from a 15-minute perspective,
the 5-minute can offer a view which gives a more clear and refined read on price throughout
the continuation.
